What's Broken?

Every piece of software has flaws. The job of a vulnerability analyst is to find those flaws systematically — before an attacker does — and turn the findings into a plan that actually gets things fixed.

🌍
Real World
The 2017 Equifax breach exposed 147 million people's data because a known, patchable vulnerability was left unfixed for months. Scanning finds the gap before that happens.
🛠️
Your Tool Today
Nessus — the industry-standard vulnerability scanner used by security teams worldwide to check systems against thousands of known CVEs.

🎯 Where this skill takes you
🔬
Vulnerability Analyst
Runs scheduled scans, triages findings by severity, and tracks remediation across an organisation's IT estate.
🕵️
Penetration Tester
Goes further than scanning — actively exploits vulnerabilities to prove real-world risk to a client.
🛠️
Security Engineer
Builds and maintains the scanning, patching and hardening systems that stop vulnerabilities piling up.
💻
AppSec Engineer
Specialises in finding and fixing vulnerabilities in custom-built software, not just off-the-shelf systems.
